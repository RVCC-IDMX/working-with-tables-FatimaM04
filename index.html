March 11th, 2011 Coast of Japan 9.1 Airports, roads and railroads destroyed, 127,290 buildings totally collapsed, 272,788 buildings half collapsed, and another 747,989 buildings partially damaged. 15,894
November 4, 1952 Kamchatka Peninsula, Russia 9.0 The waves traveled as far as Peru, Chile and New Zealand. In Alaska, the Aleutian Islands and California, tsunami waves of up to 1.4 meters (4.6 feet) were observed. 10,000 - 15,000
February 27th, 2010 Maule Region, Chile 8.8 Several coastal towns in south-central Chile were devastated by the tsunami triggered by the earthquake. The tsunami also damaged the port at Talcahuano. 525
January 31, 1906 Border between Ecuador and Colombia 8.8 The earthquake caused significant damage and destruction in the affected areas, with many buildings being destroyed and landslides blocking roads and transportation routes. 1,000
